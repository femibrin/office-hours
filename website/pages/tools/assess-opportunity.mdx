# Assess Opportunity

> Analyze a job listing: company research, fit scoring, honest assessment.

## What It Does

Assess Opportunity takes a job listing and runs three research agents in parallel: one on the company, one on the role, and one on your fit. You get a structured assessment with fit scores across four dimensions (skill match, experience match, direction match, energy match), a company snapshot, red flags, compensation context, and an honest recommendation on whether to apply.

This isn't cheerleading. A 4/10 fit is a 4/10 fit. The tool says so, explains why, and helps you decide if it's still worth pursuing.

## When to Use It

- **You found a job listing and want a quick sanity check.** Is this worth your time?
- **You're comparing multiple opportunities.** Structured scoring makes them comparable.
- **Before tailoring your resume.** Know the fit before investing the effort.

## What You'll Need

- A completed career profile (run `/baseline` first)
- A job listing — URL, pasted text, or just the company and role name
- 5-10 minutes

## How It Works

**Step 1: Share the opportunity.** Paste a URL, describe the role, or share the full JD text. The system fetches and parses it automatically.

**Step 2: Three parallel research agents launch:**
- **Company Research** — what they do, their stage, recent news, culture signals, financial health
- **Role Analysis** — what the role actually entails day-to-day, where it sits in the org, compensation benchmarks, red flags
- **Fit Assessment** — your skills, experience, career direction, and energy mapped against the requirements

**Step 3: Synthesized assessment** with a clear recommendation.

## What the Output Looks Like

| Dimension | Score | Key Signal |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Skill match | X/10 | Strongest match + biggest gap |
| Experience match | X/10 | Alignment or mismatch |
| Direction match | X/10 | Does this advance your goals? |
| Energy match | X/10 | Day-to-day alignment |
| **Overall** | **X/10** | |

Plus:
- **Company snapshot** — plain-language description, stage, recent signals
- **What works** — specific strengths mapped to specific requirements
- **What's a stretch** — gaps with strategies to address them
- **Red flags** — things to investigate, not necessarily dealbreakers
- **Compensation context** — benchmarks vs. your floor
- **The honest take** — would a good career coach say "go for it," "proceed with caution," or "skip it"?
