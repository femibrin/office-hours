# Tailor Resume

> Tailor your resume for a specific job listing. Updates bullets, not formatting.

## What It Does

Tailor Resume takes your resume and a job description, analyzes the JD for key requirements and language patterns, scores your fit, selects the best resume template (if you have multiple from `/explore-paths`), and rewrites your bullets to emphasize relevance to the specific role. Your formatting, layout, and structure stay untouched. Only the content changes.

If you've run the deep path (`/baseline` then `/explore-paths`), the system automatically picks the right template for each job listing. If you haven't, it works just as well with whatever resume you provide.

## When to Use It

- **You found a role you want to apply to.** Get a tailored resume in 5-10 minutes.
- **You're applying to multiple roles.** Run it for each one — different emphasis every time.
- **Quick path.** No profile needed. Just paste your resume and a JD.

## What You'll Need

- Your resume — pasted, file path, or already saved from a previous session
- A job listing — URL, pasted text, or company + role name
- 5-10 minutes
- A career profile is helpful but not required

## How It Works

**Step 1: Provide your resume.** If you've used Office Hours before, the system finds your saved resume or templates. If not, you'll be asked to paste or point to a file. No resume at all? There's a blank template to start from.

**Step 2: Share the job listing.** Paste a URL and it's fetched automatically. Or paste the text directly.

**Step 3: Fit scoring (if profile exists).** The system scores skill match, experience match, and direction match. If fit is below 7/10, it flags the gaps and asks if you want to proceed anyway.

**Step 4: Template selection (if multiple exist).** If you have resume templates from `/explore-paths`, the system picks the best one for this JD and explains why. You can override.

**Step 5: Edit plan.** The system presents its approach:
- Which template to use
- Edit intensity (light touch, moderate, or heavy rewrite)
- Top 3 planned changes

You approve, adjust, or give more context.

**Step 6: Tailoring.** Bullets are rewritten to emphasize relevance. Keywords are mirrored from the JD where authentic, and the most relevant experience leads each section. Skills line is updated. Formatting stays sacred.

**Step 7: Review together.** You see the result and can request adjustments on the spot.

## What the Output Looks Like

A tailored resume saved to `~/.office-hours/tailored-resumes/`, plus a customization summary:

- **Template used** and why
- **Edit intensity** applied
- **Fit score**
- **Key changes** — what changed and the reasoning
- **Keywords added** — JD terminology integrated naturally
- **The full tailored resume** in markdown

## Core Principles

- **Formatting is sacred.** Section order, fonts, margins, layout, company names, titles, dates — none of it changes.
- **Content changes only.** Bullet text, bullet order, keywords, skills line.
- **No fabrication.** Experience isn't inflated. Skills you don't have aren't added.
- **Your voice is preserved.** If your resume is direct and metrics-heavy, it stays that way. If it's narrative, that's maintained.
- **One page default.** Two pages only if you ask or have 15+ years of highly relevant experience.
