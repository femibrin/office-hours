# Baseline

> Build your career profile. Run this first — every other tool uses it.

## What It Does

Baseline builds a comprehensive career profile through a guided conversation. It extracts your experience arc, core skills, domains, seniority, career directions, strengths, energizers, drains, and non-negotiables. Everything saves locally so every other Office Hours tool starts with full context about who you are.

This isn't a form to fill out. It's a conversation. You share your background however feels natural (paste a resume, share a LinkedIn URL, point to a file, or just start talking) and the system asks smart follow-up questions to fill in the gaps.

## When to Use It

- **First time using Office Hours.** This is your starting point.
- **Career inflection point.** You're thinking about a change and want to articulate where you've been and where you could go.
- **Before any other profile-dependent tool.** Tools like `/explore-paths`, `/assess-opportunity`, and `/prep-interview` all read your profile for context. Building one makes everything smarter.

## What You'll Need

- Your resume (any format) or LinkedIn profile — or just be ready to talk through your background
- 5-10 minutes

## How It Works

**Step 1: Share your background.** You'll see options:
1. Paste my resume
2. Share a LinkedIn URL
3. Just talk through my background
4. Point to a file on my machine

Pick whichever is easiest. If you skip the menu and just start talking, that works too.

**Step 2: Follow-up questions.** The system asks 3-5 targeted questions based on what it learned — things like:
- "What do people come to you for — not your job title, but what they actually ask you about?"
- "When was the last time you were genuinely excited about your work?"
- "Any non-negotiables? Geography, comp floor, industry?"

**Step 3: Career directions.** Based on everything you've shared, the system identifies 2-3 possible career directions and asks which resonate. You pick, adjust, or describe something different.

**Step 4: Review.** You see the full profile and confirm it feels right. Anything off gets corrected on the spot.

## What the Output Looks Like

A structured career profile saved to `~/.office-hours/profile.md`, covering:

- **Experience Arc** — the narrative of your trajectory, beyond titles alone
- **Core Skills** — with evidence from your background
- **Domains** — where you have credibility (deep, moderate, or adjacent)
- **Seniority & Scope** — what you manage and your IC vs. management preference
- **Career Directions** — 2-3 self-identified paths with reasoning
- **Strengths** — including hidden ones surfaced through conversation
- **Energizers & Drains** — what lights you up and what burns you out
- **Non-Negotiables** — geography, comp, constraints
- **Open Questions** — tensions or uncertainties worth exploring
