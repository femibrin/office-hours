# Resume Review

> Get your resume reviewed. Specific feedback, not platitudes.

## What It Does

Resume Review analyzes your resume across four dimensions: content quality, structure and formatting, positioning and story, and language and polish. It delivers priority-ranked feedback with specific rewrites for every weak bullet. The feedback sounds like "Your third bullet under Google describes a duty, not an impact. Try this rewrite," never "your bullets could be stronger."

The best resume feedback is a little uncomfortable. If it's not flagging anything hard to hear, it's not being honest enough. But if your resume is genuinely strong, the tool says so. It doesn't manufacture criticism.

## When to Use It

- **Before you start applying.** Get a general quality check.
- **Targeting a specific role or industry.** Calibrated feedback for your target.
- **You know something's off but can't pinpoint it.** The tool finds it.
- **Quick path.** No profile needed. Just paste your resume.

## What You'll Need

- Your resume — pasted, file path, or already saved
- 5-10 minutes
- A career profile is helpful (adds context about your goals) but not required

## How It Works

**Step 1: Share your resume.** Paste it, point to a file, or the system finds your saved version.

**Step 2: What kind of review?**
1. **General** — just make it better
2. **Targeted** — I'm going for a specific role or industry
3. **Gut check** — is this good enough to send?
4. **Bullet surgery** — I know which sections are weak

**Step 3: Four-dimension analysis:**

**Content Quality** — For every bullet: Does it show impact or just duties? Is there a meaningful metric? Does it demonstrate judgment? Are the action verbs strong? Each bullet rated Weak / Adequate / Strong.

**Structure & Formatting** — Is the hierarchy clear? Scannable in 6 seconds (that's how long a recruiter spends)? Consistent formatting? One page? Any wasted space?

**Positioning & Story** — Does it tell a coherent story? Is there a through-line? Would a recruiter know what you're targeting within 10 seconds?

**Language & Polish** — Cliches? Consistent tense? Errors? Language calibrated to your target level?

**Step 4: Priority-ranked feedback** with specific rewrites.

## What the Output Looks Like

- **Overall rating** (X/10) with summary
- **What's working** — specific strengths with examples
- **Priority 1 fixes** — the most impactful changes, each with:
  - Current text (quoted from your resume)
  - Why it's weak
  - Suggested rewrite
- **Priority 2 fixes** — important but less urgent
- **Priority 3 fixes** — polish
- **Structural recommendations** — layout or ordering changes
- **Missing content** — things that should be added
- **The one-sentence version** — "If I could only fix one thing, it would be X, because Y"

## Example Usage

```
/resume-review
```

```
/resume-review ~/Documents/my-resume.pdf
```

After the review, you can ask the system to rewrite specific bullets on the spot or generate a revised version of the full resume.
