# Deep Dive Role

> Go deep on a specific role, team, or company. Research-heavy, no resume output.

## What It Does

Deep Dive Role runs five parallel research streams to give you a thorough picture of a company, team, or specific position. It covers the company's mission and trajectory, team leadership and recent work, culture and employee experience, role trajectory and promotion paths, and market context including compensation benchmarks.

This is pure research: no fit scoring, no resume tailoring. It's for when you want to understand a place before making any decisions.

## When to Use It

- **You're interviewing somewhere and want to really understand the company.** Go beyond the career page.
- **You're deciding between offers.** Get the full picture on each.
- **You're curious about a company or function.** Explore without commitment.
- **Before or after `/assess-opportunity`.** Assessment gives you fit scores; deep dive gives you context.

## What You'll Need

- A company name, role title, or job listing URL
- A career profile is helpful but not required — this tool works standalone
- 10-15 minutes

## How It Works

**Step 1: What to focus on.** You choose:
1. The team — who runs it, what they've shipped
2. The culture — what it's actually like to work there
3. The trajectory — where this role leads in 2-3 years
4. The market — how this compares to similar roles elsewhere
5. All of the above (takes longer but more thorough)

**Step 2: Five parallel research streams launch:**
- **Company Deep Dive** — mission, products, leadership, recent news, competitors
- **Team & Org Research** — who leads the team, what they've shipped, open roles (growth vs. backfill signals)
- **Culture & Employee Experience** — Glassdoor patterns, Blind posts, LinkedIn signals, remote policy, DEI
- **Role Trajectory** — where people in this role go next, promotion path, transferable vs. company-specific skills
- **Market Context** — comp benchmarks, peer companies, function growth trends

**Step 3: Synthesized report.** Specific people to know about, sourced findings, and a clear picture of the opportunity.

## What the Output Looks Like

A comprehensive research document covering:

- **Company Overview** — what they do, where they're going (no marketing language)
- **Leadership** — CEO, team lead, and what leadership signals about direction
- **The Team** — what they own, what they've shipped, team size, open roles
- **The Role** — what you'd actually do day-to-day (stripped of JD language)
- **Culture** — consistent signals, watch-outs, work style, DEI
- **Where This Role Leads** — typical next steps, skills built, alumni trajectory
- **Market Context** — comp range, peer companies, market trends
- **Key People** — hiring manager, skip-level, team thought leaders
- **How This Maps to Your Background** (if you have a profile) — strongest connections, growth areas, positioning angle

All findings are sourced with dates so you know how current the information is.
