# Prep Interview

> Multi-agent interview prep: company research, role analysis, email search, and tailored questions.

## What It Does

Prep Interview launches four research agents in parallel: company deep dive, role analysis, email correspondence search (if Gmail is connected), and question generation with STAR-format response outlines drawn from your actual experience. The output is a full prep document plus a one-page quick reference card designed to be reviewed 5 minutes before the call.

Speed matters here. People run this the night before or morning of. The system gets to the prep doc fast.

## When to Use It

- **You have an interview coming up.** Phone screen, technical, panel, final — any stage.
- **You want structured prep that goes beyond "research the company."** Likely questions, response outlines, fit narrative, and talking points.
- **You want to feel prepared without spending 3 hours on it.** 5-10 minutes.

## What You'll Need

- A completed career profile (run `/baseline` first)
- The company and role name, or a job listing URL
- Interview details — when, what stage, who you're meeting with (if known)
- 5-10 minutes
- Optional: Gmail MCP connection (for recruiter email search)

## How It Works

**Step 1: Share the details.** Company, role, interview date, stage (phone screen, panel, etc.), and who you're meeting with if you know.

**Step 2: Four parallel research agents launch:**

- **Agent 1: Company Deep Dive** — mission, products, recent news, culture signals, competitors. Produces specific talking points you can reference.
- **Agent 2: Role Analysis** — JD parsing, team ownership, hiring manager (LinkedIn), similar roles at peer companies. Produces a fit narrative mapping your experience to requirements.
- **Agent 3: Email Search** (if Gmail connected) — searches for recruiter correspondence, scheduling details, comp mentions, and context the recruiter shared about the role.
- **Agent 4: Questions & Talking Points** — generates 8-10 likely questions (behavioral, technical, situational) with STAR-format response outlines using specific examples from your background. Plus 3-5 questions for you to ask them.

**Step 3: Full prep document** with everything synthesized, plus a quick reference card.

## What the Output Looks Like

### Full Prep Document
- **Recruiter Correspondence** — what was discussed, comp mentions, context
- **Company Overview** — with specific talking points to reference
- **The Role** — what you'd actually do, team context, success metrics
- **Your Fit Narrative** — the one-sentence story connecting your background to this role
- **Likely Questions** (8-10) — each with: why they ask, your angle, and a STAR response outline
- **Questions to Ask Them** — specific to this company and role, not generic

### Quick Reference Card
Designed to fit on one screen. Review it 5 minutes before the call:

| | |
|-|-|
| **Company** | One-line description |
| **Role** | Title + team |
| **Interviewer(s)** | Names if known |
| **Your "Why this company"** | One sentence |
| **Your "Why this role"** | One sentence |
| **Top 3 talking points** | Key things to hit |
| **Your question for them** | Your best question |
| **Concern to address** | Biggest gap + framing |

## What to Do Next

After reviewing the prep doc, the system offers:

1. **Practice a specific question** — drill on it with back-and-forth
2. **Adjust the fit narrative** — reframe how you're positioning yourself
3. **Add a specific story** — include an example you want to use
4. **Generate more questions** — for a specific interviewer
5. **You're ready** — go nail it
