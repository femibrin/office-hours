# Career Board

> Assemble a personal board of directors for a career decision. Structured deliberation, not generic advice.

## What It Does

Career Board creates a panel of 5-7 AI advisors, each with a distinct analytical lens, and has them independently research and deliberate on your career decision. They disagree where it matters. The output is a structured synthesis: where the board agrees, where they clash, and a clear recommendation with conditions.

Every board member has a research mandate. No opinions without evidence. Generic advice is banned.

## When to Use It

- **You're torn on a big decision.** Take this offer or stay? Pivot or double down?
- **You want structured pushback.** Not encouragement — real challenge to your assumptions.
- **You're navigating something complex.** Multiple variables, unclear tradeoffs, high stakes.

## What You'll Need

- A completed career profile (run `/baseline` first)
- A clear description of the decision or situation
- 10-15 minutes
- Optionally: a job listing, offer details, or other context

## How It Works

**Step 1: Describe your situation.** The system asks a few deepening questions. What a great outcome looks like, what keeps you up at night, what kind of pushback would actually help.

**Step 2: The system classifies your moment** and selects the right board composition:

| Moment | Board Composition |
|--------|-------------------|
| Path Check | Domain Expert + Skeptic + Pattern Matcher + Mirror |
| Offer Evaluation | Negotiator + Skeptic + Narrator + Connector + Compass |
| Pivot Planning | Bridge Builder + Domain Expert + Pattern Matcher + Narrator |
| Negotiation | Negotiator + Skeptic + Mirror + Connector |
| Direction Finding | Compass + Mirror + Pattern Matcher + Narrator |

You can adjust the board before it starts.

**Step 3: Choose your format:**
1. **Full Board (5-7 members)** — big decisions, offer evaluations
2. **Duo (2 members)** — quick gut-check, two competing perspectives
3. **Sprint Check (3 members)** — weekly accountability: what moved, what stalled

**Step 4: Parallel deliberation.** All board members launch simultaneously. Each one researches independently, forms a position (for/against/conditional), and produces an evidence-backed analysis with a specific pushback question.

**Step 5: Synthesis** — areas of agreement, where they clash (with quotes), and a clear recommendation.

## The Board Members

| Persona | Lens | Recurring Question |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| **Pattern Matcher** | Career archetypes and market patterns | "What usually happens when someone in your position makes this choice?" |
| **Skeptic** | Hidden risks, stress-testing assumptions | "What's the version of this that goes wrong?" |
| **Narrator** | Positioning, visibility, career story | "How does this chapter read to the people who matter?" |
| **Connector** | Relationships, network effects | "Who do you gain access to — and who do you lose?" |
| **Mirror** | Values alignment, internal consistency | "Does this match what you said mattered to you?" |
| **Compass** | Long-term direction, life alignment | "In 10 years, which version of you is happiest?" |
| **Negotiator** | Compensation, leverage, tactics | "What's your walk-away number, and do you mean it?" |
| **Bridge Builder** | Transition planning, gap closing | "What's the shortest credible path from here to there?" |
| **Domain Expert** | Deep knowledge of the target world | "What do insiders actually value vs. what outsiders assume?" |

The Domain Expert adapts to your context. A nonprofit pivot gets nonprofit expertise, a tech career gets engineering culture expertise.

## What the Output Looks Like

- **Board summary table** — each member's position (for/against/conditional) and confidence level
- **Where they agree** — consensus points with reasoning
- **Where they clash** — direct quotes from opposing members with implications
- **Synthesis** — the moderator's integration of all perspectives
- **Recommendation** — clear, with conditions
- **What to do this week** — 3 specific actions
- **The question you should sit with** — one reflective question without an easy answer
